Health and Safety Protocol
To ensure the health and safety of the community, you must read this protocol before you can volunteer.
Volunteers
To be a volunteer, you have to:
  • Be between ages 18-40
  • Be healthy without pre-existing health conditions that may increase your risk of severe illness, such as severe asthma, respiratory diseases, heart disease, diabetes
  • Be without fever, cough, short of breath, and flu-like symptoms (excessive fatigue, muscle or joint pain)
  • Have not been exposed to individuals tested positive for COVID-19 within the last 2 weeks
  • Have been practicing social distancing
Delivery protocol precautions
Your safety is paramount during this process. We can only help others if we are taking care of ourselves as well.
Communicate with the requester through phone/email as much as possible while minimizing face-to-face contact.
While coronaviruses spread mostly through respiratory droplets (sneezing, coughing), the virus can also be on surfaces through contact. When aerosolized, the virus remains viable for up to 3 hours. On surfaces, the viruses can survive for up to 4 hours on copper, 24 hours on cardboard, and 72 hours on plastic and stainless steel.
To disinfect surfaces, effective methods include: Clorox/Lysol/other antiviral wipes, other liquids containing at least 60-70% alcohol, or 0.5% hydrogen peroxide. Dilute 1:100 bleach solutions (1 part 5% sodium hypochlorite bleach to 99 parts cold water) are cheap and effective.
Please certify that you have read the Health and Safety protocol.
